What is Vaping?

Vaping is the act of inhaling a vapor created by an electronic cigarette or other device. The vapor usually contains nicotine, flavoring, and other harmful chemicals. It was originally designed to help adults quit smoking, but it's now become a growing problem among teens.

  • Lung Injuries: Vaping has been linked to serious lung disease and breathing issues.
  • Heart Problems: E-cigarettes can raise your heart rate and blood pressure, increasing your risk of heart attack.
  • Chemical Exposure: Vape aerosols contain harmful substances like formaldehyde and heavy metals.

Why Teens Are at Risk

Teen brains are still developing, making them more vulnerable to addiction. Vaping affects attention, learning, and mental health. Peer pressure, cool flavors, and misleading ads make vaping appealingâ€”but the consequences are serious and long-lasting.

Vaping Statistics

  • Over 2.5 million U.S. students currently use e-cigarettes (CDC, 2022).
  • 85% of teen vapers use flavored products.
  • Nicotine exposure in teens increases risk of future addiction.
  • More than 1 in 5 high school students vape regularly.
